Petra Cortright, '470 nitro express', 2014, Steve Turner | Artsy

Petra Cortright uses a range of mediums, both digital and analog, to explore the aesthetics and performative cultures of online consumption. She is particularly interested in video-sharing sites like Youtube: in (2007), for example, Cortright recorded herself on a webcam passively scrolling through different effects on the recording device, while a repetitive trance beat played in the background.

Video: Discovery: The Ming Dynasty 'Dragon' jar that was used as an umbrella stand

'The owner had always thought this was an 18th-century jar, a decorative object,' explains Marco Almeida, a specialist in Chinese Ceramics and Works of Art at Christie's in London. This magnificent and very rare large blue and white 'Dragon' jar was being used as an umbrella stand when Almeida got in touch with the owner, who agreed to send it to London to be inspected.

Rumblings in the Auction World

A year or two ago, in less unsettled times, it was relatively easy for the wealthy to enhance their social status, and maybe make some money, by collecting art. Auction houses courted sellers with lavish guarantees; buyers, even at the top of the market, could be reasonably certain that quality works by blue-chip names would hold their value.
