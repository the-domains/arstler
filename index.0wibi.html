William Betts, 'Sunbathers, Miami Beach', 2016, Margaret Thatcher Projects | Artsy

William Betts uses painting to explore images in the age of surveillance. He creates his work like a pointillist painter, carefully applying hundreds of acrylic dots to canvas to mimic the pixelated images captured by CCTV, or translating a single row of pixels from a digital photo into abstractions with an uncannily organic quality.
